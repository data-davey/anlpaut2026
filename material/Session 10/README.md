# Session 10 — AI Security, Safety, and Responsible Deployment

**Date:** 4 May 2026

## Overview

Session 10 closes the ANLP course by connecting NLP and LLM techniques to responsible deployment. The session is anchored by an industry guest lecture from Julian Lee:

> **AI without Security is a Liability**

The session focuses on how organizations should think about AI security, AI safety, ethics, and social impact when deploying LLM, RAG, and agentic systems.

## Guest Lecture

**Speaker:** Julian Lee

### Agenda

1. AI is a double-edged sword
2. We have done safety and security before
3. Discover, Assess, and Protect demo
4. Thoughts on the future
5. Q&A

### Speaker Bio

Julian Lee began his career in cancer research during the post-Dolly the sheep and Human Genome Project era. He argues that genomic data predates Big Data and is deeply statistical in nature.

While completing his Master's in Statistics, Julian founded the R User Group in Singapore and was later recruited as the first pre-sales Data Scientist at a startup acquired by Microsoft in 2015. Since then, he has seen AI reborn multiple times while helping drive AI adoption in the enterprise.

Today, Julian says he atones for his AI sins by making sure AI is safer and more secure.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, students should be able to:

- explain why AI security is a practical requirement for deployed AI systems
- distinguish between AI safety, AI security, ethics, and governance
- identify security risks in LLM, RAG, and agentic workflows
- describe a Discover, Assess, and Protect approach to AI security
- explain why frontier AI labs now use philosophy, governance, and safety research as part of model design
- reflect on responsible AI deployment from an industry perspective
